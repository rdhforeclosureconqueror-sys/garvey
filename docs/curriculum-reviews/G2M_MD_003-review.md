# G2M_MD_003 — Data, Graphs, and Line Plots Publication Review

## Sequence selection and scope

**Selected package: `G2M_MD_003`. Publication decision: certified for production publication.** The authoritative Grade 2 Math completion plan places `G2M_MD_003 — Data, Graphs, and Line Plots` immediately after the approved `G2M_MD_002 — Time and Money`, and the production content manifest exposes that same package identity. Therefore this audit selected the immediate next canonical package and did not skip to `G2M_GM_001` or open any second package.

This was a fresh, exhaustive review of all 50 canonical activities: ten in each of four focused banks and ten in Mixed. All IDs are unique, every `id` matches `question_id`, and the package validates against the production SkillPackage schema without planned-bank allowances. Canonical preview copies in guided practice, adaptive practice, and checkpoint were synchronized exactly to their level-bank sources.

## Educational review

Every activity was individually rewritten or remediated for clear Grade 2 language, concrete labels, purposeful progression, and distinct teaching. Picture graphs begin with reading labels and keys, bar graphs progress through reading and comparing heights, tables emphasize aligned rows, and line plots progress from counting a stack to totals, modes, differences, and extrema. Prompts, graph titles, contexts, data arrangements, and full hint ladders are unique.

Each activity has exactly one activity-specific **Focus**, **Strategy**, and **Verify** hint. Explanations name the displayed values or X-mark counts and teach the operation or comparison that connects those data to the conclusion; none merely states that an answer is correct.

## Mathematical and metadata review

Every category count, picture-graph scale, difference, combined total, greatest-category result, symbol-group count, line-plot frequency, total mark count, mode, and extreme was recomputed. Picture-graph totals divide evenly by their authored key, line-plot bounds equal their actual minimum and maximum, and every X corresponds to one authored measurement. `answer` and `correct_answer` agree, intentional acceptable answers evaluate, and unrelated responses are rejected.

Multiple-choice activities have four unique synchronized `options` and `choices`, always contain the canonical answer, and distribute correct answers across all four positions. Production submission tests verify incorrect-state recording, misconception updates, retry behavior, correct-state updates, and attempt counts.

## Mixed transfer review

All ten Mixed activities were rewritten from scratch. They use fair booths, park cleanup, picnic orders, shell lengths, museum favorites, bus riders, a bake sale, ribbon lengths, a talent show, and pencil lengths—none reused from a focused bank. Mixed data sets, values, graph titles, visual arrangements, prompts, and hint ladders are also fresh.

Every Mixed prompt explicitly asks the learner to choose the useful graph-reading method before solving. Across the bank, learners decide whether to read a labeled value, count marks, compare by subtraction, combine counts, use a picture key, identify a category, or locate a greatest recorded measurement.

## Visual and accessibility review

All 50 activities were rendered deterministically through the production visual registry and the complete production question-card renderer. The full production Skill World presentation path was also rendered fail-closed. Checks confirm the intended `picture_graph`, `bar_graph`, `data_table`, or `line_plot` renderer; nonblank output; all authored titles and labels; exact X-mark totals; valid picture keys; and no unsupported visual, visual placeholder, missing renderer, or solution-statement leakage. Shared renderer contract suites passed, so no reusable renderer or infrastructure defect was discovered.

Every activity includes a natural-language `visual_description`, matching `accessible_description`, and explicit **Read Question** audio whose text starts with the complete prompt and includes meaningful visual structure. Descriptions identify graph type, title, categories or number range, label arrangement, key/scale where relevant, and how marks are organized without declaring the solution.

## Browser verification

The Playwright CLI was available only through an on-demand package fetch, but no Chromium or other supported browser executable was installed in the environment. Consequently no browser screenshots were captured or claimed. Every canonical activity was instead checked deterministically through both applicable production HTML rendering paths, plus the full fail-closed Skill World presentation path.
