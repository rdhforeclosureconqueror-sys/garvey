## Questions answered

1. **What assessments actually exist beyond `business_owner` and `customer`?** Love, Leadership, Loyalty archetype engines; Youth Development; Gates / Youth Rite of Passage; K–6 Assessment MVP; Skill World/Gamehub practice and checkpoint-like content.
2. **Which assessment routes are production-ready?** Business Owner and Customer/VOC are the most production-ready because they already have public pages, DB-backed questions, scoring, storage, and dashboards. Archetype engines are functional but should be treated as preview/member-only until content/consent is approved. Gates/K–6 are production-shaped but parent/child-auth dependent.
3. **Which should Simba expose first?** Business Owner and Customer/VOC first. Love/Leadership/Loyalty can be preview cards. Youth/Gates/K–6 should stay parent-only until a Simba parent/child identity bridge exists.
4. **Which role should Simba users use?** Use existing `customer` for MVP to avoid broad business-owner dashboard permissions. Do not add a destructive new auth role until route access rules require it.
5. **Should all Simba users live under one tenant?** Yes for MVP: `SIMBAWAJUMAA_DEFAULT_TENANT` (default `simbawajuma`). This aligns with Garvey's tenant-scoped data model and avoids cross-tenant leakage.
6. **Which results can be sent back safely?** Completion summary, provider IDs, Garvey user id, assessment type/name, result/submission id, primary archetype/result, points awarded, completion timestamp, and result URL. Do not send raw answers by default.
7. **Which completions award points?** Customer/VOC awards 50 points when reward points are enabled. Business Owner and archetype engine completions do not show standard Garvey point awards.
8. **Can Youth/Rite of Passage be used without Gates parent auth?** Not safely for MVP. Gates and Assessment MVP require parent/child ownership context; Simba needs a separate parent/child identity mapping before those are exposed.
