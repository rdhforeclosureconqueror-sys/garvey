# K–6 Math Curriculum Coverage Audit

## Executive Summary
- **Overall coverage estimate**: Grade 1 ~70% complete against full CCSS-like scope; Grade 2 ~75%; Grade 3 ~80%; Grade 4 ~78%; Grade 5 ~72%; Grade 6 ~85%.
- **Biggest gaps**: Grade 1 place value to 120 and operations within 100 depth; Grade 4 lacks formal factors/multiples and fraction × whole number; Grade 5 lacks full coordinate graphing and measurement conversions; Grade 6 lacks rational number operations depth.
- **Highest-priority fixes**: (1) Complete Grade 1 standards-critical place value/operation/data/time/geometry partitions; (2) strengthen Grades 2–3 foundations for fluency and fraction equivalence; (3) close Grade 4–6 structural CCSS domain gaps (factors/multiples, full numerical expressions, rational number system).

## Cross-Grade Progression Issues
- **NBT progression breaks**: Grade 3 has insufficient explicit NBT strand after Grade 2 strength.
- **Fractions progression uneven**: Grade 2 nearly absent; Grade 3 foundational; Grade 4–5 heavy, causing readiness risk.
- **Data progression fragmented**: Graph interpretation exists, but measurement-data integration (line plots/fractional units) is inconsistent.
- **Algebraic thinking pacing**: Equation reasoning appears in Grade 1 and reappears strongly in Grade 6; Grades 3–5 need clearer bridge through pattern generalization and variable-prep structures.
