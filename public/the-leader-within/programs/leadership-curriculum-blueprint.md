# Leadership Curriculum Blueprint
## 1. Program philosophy
Leadership is not only a title. Leadership is a helpful behavior practiced in real situations. Participants learn to observe leadership, practice it, reflect on it, adjust, and eventually help others develop it too. The curriculum should not permanently label participants. It asks: “What leadership capacities appeared?” rather than only “Who is the leader?”
The five capacities are Vision Drive, Structure Drive, Relational Intelligence, Influence Expression, and Adaptive Control. They are lenses for behavior, practice, observation, reflection, and growth.
## 2. Shared leadership progression
See Leadership → Lead Yourself → Lead With Others → Build Strong Groups → Develop New Leaders → Create Lasting Systems
## 3. 32-week full curriculum map
### Phase 1: See Leadership (Weeks 1–8)
Focus: recognizing leadership, noticing needs, naming capacities, observing group behavior, and practicing simple helpful actions.
| Week | Title | Core question | Main lesson | Capacity emphasis | Practice focus | Reflection focus | Builds from previous week |
|---:|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Leadership Is Already Happening | Where is leadership already showing up in my life? | Leadership begins when someone notices what is needed and chooses a helpful response. | All five capacities | Notice ordinary leadership moments and choose one practice. | What leadership capacities appeared? | Establishes leadership as practiced behavior, not title or label. |
| 2 | Notice Needs | What is needed here? | Helpful leadership starts with careful noticing before action. | Relational Intelligence | Observe a group, name one need, and choose one helpful response. | What did I notice before acting? | Moves from seeing leadership to seeing the need that invites leadership. |
| 3 | Name the Capacity | What kind of leadership response appeared? | Participants learn shared language for vision, structure, relationship, influence, and adaptability. | All five capacities | Match real actions to capacities without labeling people. | Which capacity helped, and which was missing? | Builds vocabulary for what was noticed in Week 2. |
| 4 | See Patterns | What keeps happening in this group? | Leaders look beyond one moment to repeated habits, roles, and gaps. | Vision Drive | Track one repeating pattern across the week. | What pattern did I see over time? | Extends noticing from single needs to repeated group behavior. |
| 5 | Helpful Action | What small action would help now? | Leadership can be a simple next helpful step, not a dramatic rescue. | Structure Drive | Take one small, useful action and leave space for others. | Did my action help the group move? | Turns observation and patterns into first action. |
| 6 | Balanced Strength | When does a strength become too loud? | Every capacity can help when balanced and crowd others when overused. | Adaptive Control | Use a familiar strength with one balancing behavior. | Where did my strength help or crowd the group? | Adds discernment to the helpful action from Week 5. |
| 7 | Group Signals | How does a group show what it needs? | Participation, silence, confusion, energy, and tension are information. | Relational Intelligence | Watch body language, participation, and tone before deciding. | What did the group signal? | Expands individual observation into whole-group awareness. |
| 8 | Practice With Choice | Which capacity does this moment need? | Mature leadership chooses a response instead of reacting automatically. | All five capacities | Try one less-familiar leadership response. | What did I choose instead of doing automatically? | Completes Phase 1 by combining observation, naming, balance, and choice. |

### Phase 2: Lead Yourself (Weeks 9–16)
Focus: self-awareness, emotional regulation, discipline, consistency, decision-making, feedback, responsibility, and personal growth.
| Week | Title | Core question | Main lesson | Capacity emphasis | Practice focus | Reflection focus | Builds from previous week |
|---:|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 9 | Lead Yourself First | How do I guide myself before guiding others? | Self-leadership begins with awareness of energy, emotion, and choice. | Adaptive Control | Pause, breathe, and choose one response. | What happened inside me before I acted? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 10 | Emotional Regulation | How can I stay steady without shutting down? | Regulation protects the group from reactive leadership. | Adaptive Control | Use a reset before a difficult conversation or task. | What helped me stay steady? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 11 | Discipline and Follow-Through | Can people trust my commitments? | Consistency turns good intentions into trustworthy action. | Structure Drive | Complete one small promise or repair one miss. | What did follow-through require? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 12 | Decision-Making | What choice serves the goal and the people? | Good decisions consider purpose, people, constraints, and consequences. | Vision Drive | Use a simple choice map: goal, options, impact, next step. | How did I decide? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 13 | Feedback Without Shame | What can feedback teach me? | Feedback is information for growth, not proof of identity. | Relational Intelligence | Ask for one observation and one suggestion. | What did I learn that I could not see alone? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 14 | Responsibility Without Control | What is mine to carry, and what is not? | Responsible leaders act clearly without owning everyone else’s choices. | Structure Drive | Name your role, next action, and boundary. | Did I help without controlling? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 15 | Repair and Accountability | How do I respond after harm, mistake, or missed commitment? | Leadership includes repair, apology, adjustment, and recommitment. | Relational Intelligence | Make one specific repair or reset plan. | What changed after I repaired? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 16 | Personal Growth Plan | What practice will help me keep growing? | Growth becomes stronger when it becomes a repeated practice. | Vision Drive | Create a two-week personal leadership practice plan. | What pattern am I intentionally building? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |

### Phase 3: Lead Others (Weeks 17–24)
Focus: communication, trust, coaching, delegation, shared responsibility, conflict, group coordination, and helping others contribute.
| Week | Title | Core question | Main lesson | Capacity emphasis | Practice focus | Reflection focus | Builds from previous week |
|---:|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 17 | Build Trust | How do people learn they are safe to contribute? | Trust grows through honesty, consistency, respect, and care. | Relational Intelligence | Do one trust-building action. | What increased or reduced trust? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 18 | Communicate Clearly | How can my words help people understand and participate? | Clear communication joins speaking, listening, timing, and confirmation. | Influence Expression | Explain one idea and check understanding. | What became clearer? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 19 | Coach, Do Not Rescue | How do I help someone grow without taking over? | Coaching supports another person’s ownership. | Relational Intelligence | Ask before advising; offer one prompt, not the whole answer. | Did the other person keep responsibility? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 20 | Delegate With Clarity | How do we share real responsibility? | Delegation requires role clarity, support, and trust. | Structure Drive | Give or accept a role with a clear outcome. | Was responsibility truly shared? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 21 | Navigate Disagreement | How can conflict become useful instead of harmful? | Healthy conflict names needs, protects respect, and seeks next steps. | Adaptive Control | Practice a slow disagreement script. | What did the disagreement reveal? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 22 | Coordinate Action | How do separate efforts become one shared effort? | Coordination aligns goals, roles, timing, and communication. | Structure Drive | Build a simple shared action board. | What helped the group move together? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 23 | Shared Responsibility | How does leadership move through the group? | Strong groups do not depend on one person carrying everything. | All five capacities | Rotate who names goal, steps, inclusion, energy, and adjustment. | Where did leadership move? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 24 | Help Others Contribute | What makes contribution easier for different people? | Leaders create conditions for different capacities to appear. | Influence Expression | Invite one underused voice or capacity into the work. | Who contributed because space was made? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |

### Phase 4: Build Communities (Weeks 25–32)
Focus: service, culture, systems, mutual aid, institution-building, succession, accountability, and multiplying leadership beyond the individual.
| Week | Title | Core question | Main lesson | Capacity emphasis | Practice focus | Reflection focus | Builds from previous week |
|---:|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 25 | Service Lens | Who are we helping, and what do they actually need? | Community leadership begins with listening and service. | Relational Intelligence | Ask or research what a real audience needs. | Did we serve an actual need or our assumption? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 26 | Culture Is Repeated Behavior | What behaviors are becoming normal here? | Culture is built by repeated choices, not slogans. | Structure Drive | Name one behavior to reinforce and one to interrupt. | What are we normalizing? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 27 | Mutual Aid and Support | How do we help each other without creating dependence? | Mutual aid pairs care with dignity, boundaries, and shared contribution. | Relational Intelligence | Create one support exchange with roles and limits. | Did support preserve dignity and agency? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 28 | Build Systems | What routine or structure would make this easier next time? | Systems help good behavior outlast mood, memory, and individual effort. | Structure Drive | Document one repeatable routine. | What can work without me reminding everyone? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 29 | Accountability Practices | How do we know we are doing what we said? | Accountability is shared review, honest evidence, and adjustment. | Adaptive Control | Run a brief check-in: promise, evidence, obstacle, next step. | What did accountability reveal? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 30 | Develop New Leaders | How do I help someone else practice leadership? | Multiplying leadership means coaching observation, practice, and reflection in others. | Influence Expression | Help another participant choose and reflect on a practice. | What growth did I support in someone else? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 31 | Succession and Continuity | What happens when the original leader steps back? | Lasting groups prepare others to carry purpose and routines forward. | Vision Drive | Teach one role or process to someone else. | What can continue without me? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |
| 32 | Lasting Leadership Story | What did we build that can keep helping? | Participants connect personal growth, group learning, and durable contribution. | All five capacities | Share a story, system, and next leadership handoff. | What should continue, change, or multiply? | Builds from the previous week by adding the next layer of practice and responsibility. |

## 4. 12-week core curriculum map
| Week | Title | Core question | Main lesson | Practice focus | Story focus | Reflection focus | Growth outcome |
|---:|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Leadership Is Already Happening | Where is leadership already showing up in my life? | Observe leadership | Notice ordinary leadership without title or rank. | Five short stories show different capacities appearing in daily life. | What leadership capacities appeared? | Participants can identify leadership as practiced behavior. |
| 2 | Notice Needs | What is needed here? | Develop awareness | Name one need before acting. | A group moment where someone notices confusion, exclusion, or pressure. | What did I notice before I chose a response? | Participants slow down and observe needs. |
| 3 | See Patterns | What keeps happening over time? | Think beyond the moment | Track repeated habits, roles, or gaps. | A recurring group pattern that needs a better response. | What pattern is asking for leadership? | Participants connect moments into patterns. |
| 4 | Build Trust | How do people learn they can contribute safely? | Strengthen relationships | Practice consistency, listening, and respect. | A peer trust moment where honesty and care matter. | What built or weakened trust? | Participants see trust as the base for group work. |
| 5 | Navigate Disagreement | How can conflict become useful? | Practice healthy conflict | Use a respectful disagreement script. | A team disagreement with real needs underneath. | What did disagreement help us understand? | Participants practice tension without harm. |
| 6 | Coordinate Action | How do we organize people and responsibilities? | Organize people and responsibilities | Clarify goal, roles, timing, and next steps. | A project that only works when roles are clear. | What helped the group move together? | Participants turn trust and conflict skill into action. |
| 7 | Share Responsibility | How do I help without rescuing or controlling? | Help without rescuing or controlling | Give real responsibility with support. | A helper learns not to take over. | Did responsibility stay shared? | Participants learn shared ownership. |
| 8 | Adapt Under Pressure | How do I stay steady when plans change? | Stay steady when plans change | Name the goal, adjust the plan, communicate clearly. | An event changes suddenly and the group must recover. | What stayed steady, and what changed? | Participants add adaptability to shared action. |
| 9 | Develop Another Leader | How do I encourage growth in someone else? | Coach and encourage growth | Ask, encourage, and reflect with another person. | One participant helps another choose a practice. | What growth did I support? | Participants move from self-practice to coaching. |
| 10 | Build Systems | What routines help leadership last? | Create routines and structures that last | Document one repeatable process. | A group creates a routine that prevents repeated confusion. | What can continue without one person carrying it? | Participants convert helpful behavior into structure. |
| 11 | Reflect and Grow | What can feedback and experience teach me? | Learn from feedback and experience | Review practice, feedback, and adjustment. | A participant revisits an early moment with new eyes. | What changed because I practiced? | Participants consolidate learning. |
| 12 | Multiply Leadership | How does leadership move through the group? | Help leadership move through the group | Plan a handoff, shared routine, or next practice circle. | A group succeeds because leadership is distributed. | What should continue and who can carry it next? | Participants finish with multiplication, not personal status. |

## 6. How the three versions relate
The 32-week program is the full school-year pathway. The 12-week program is the core sequence that preserves the same developmental arc with fewer repetitions. The 8-week program is a foundation track that compresses observation, trust, communication, conflict, teamwork, adaptation, and multiplication into one cycle. Week One remains the common starting point in all three versions.

## 7. Voice/audio guidance
Garvey AI Voice should read weekly introductions, story cards, key lesson summaries, practice explanations, and reflection guidance. Garvey AI Voice should not read facilitator notes, developer notes, technical integration notes, PocketPT implementation notes, completion metadata, admin-only language, or HTML labels. Each weekly story should be treated like a short audio track. Recommended weekly audio structure: Track 1 weekly introduction; Tracks 2–6 stories 1–5; Track 7 what leadership appeared; Track 8 choose your practice; Track 9 reflection. Do not build live voice-response coaching yet; this blueprint only marks where it could fit later.
